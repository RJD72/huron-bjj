Coaches

Shaun Garrity

Shaun Garrity, the owner and founder of Huron BJJ, has been a dedicated student of Jorge Britto for over a decade. His journey began at MTC in Windsor, where he honed his skills before moving to Stratford BJJ. Shaun's passion for Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu and his commitment to the sport led him to open his own gym, Huron BJJ, where he has cultivated a strong community of practitioners. Under Shaun's guidance, Huron BJJ has become a place where students of all ages can develop their skills, learn the values of respect and discipline, and strive for excellence both on and off the mats.

Jeff Hakkers

Jeff Hakkers, a blue belt at Huron BJJ, is our dedicated Lead Youth Coach alongside Shaun Garrity. Jeff has been a vital part of our morning coaching team since joining us in 2019, following his children's enrollment. Known for his commitment and passion, Jeff has also stepped into the competition scene, earning a handful of bronze and silver medals. His experience and enthusiasm make him a great asset to our team and a positive influence on our young athletes!

Wyatt Carey

Wyatt Carey is a cornerstone of our youth Brazilian Jiu Jitsu program and serves as a Lead Coach alongside Shaun Garrity. Wyatt began his BJJ journey in 2019 at another gym and became a valuable member of our team in 2021. A natural-born athlete, Wyatt brings a wealth of energy and enthusiasm to his coaching. Outside of BJJ, he’s a dedicated volunteer firefighter, balancing his passion for martial arts with his commitment to serving the community. Wyatt’s dedication and diverse skills make him an inspiring role model for our young practitioners.

SK Fenton

SK Fenton is a purple belt who has been a vital member of our gym since 2018. She joined shortly after her boys started training and has never looked back. SK leads our popular Sunday program, which includes Adult BJJ and Active Recovery—a unique blend of flow, strength training, and yoga. Known for her dedication, SK expertly balances home life, work, family, and Jiu-Jitsu, making her an inspiring example for our community. She also holds an impressive gold medal from the Ontario Open as a blue belt, the biggest tournament in the country.

Nancy Hamelin

Nancy Hamelin, a purple belt who has been training since 2019, is the head instructor of our Women’s Only program. She brings her extensive experience and deep passion for Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu to every class she leads. Nancy creates a positive and supportive environment that empowers women to excel in BJJ. In addition to her coaching, Nancy has an impressive competitive medal, a gold medal at the Ontario Open as a blue belt—the biggest tournament in the country. Her unique coaching style not only focuses on developing physical skills but also emphasizes mental resilience and discipline, helping her students reach their full potential both on and off the mats. Nancy's commitment to her students and her passion for the sport make her an invaluable part of our training team.
